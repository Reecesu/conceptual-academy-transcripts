McClintock and Baker observed something unusual: a large percentage of amphipods were carrying sea butterflies on their backs, with the sea butterflies held tightly by the legs of the amphipods. Often this is done by running an experimental test along with a control. Ideally, the experimental test and the control should differ by only one variable. Any differences in results can then be attributed to that single variable.

Because of this potential for unseen error, experimental results must be reproducible to be considered valid in the scientific community.
