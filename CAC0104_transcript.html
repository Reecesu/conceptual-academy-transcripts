Let me summarize what we mean by these terms:
- A scientific fact is an agreed upon truth about the natural world. For example: Wood burns.
- A scientific hypothesis is a testable explanation for an observable phenomenon. For example: Wood transforms mostly to gaseous materials as it burns.
- A scientific law is an experimentally confirmed description of the natural world. For example: The mass of the products of a reaction equals the mass of the reactants.
- A scientific theory is a well-tested explanation that unifies many observations. For example: Matter is made of tiny particles called atoms that are not destroyed during a chemical transformation.
