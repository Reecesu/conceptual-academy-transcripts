Yes, it does. Heisenberg found that when the uncertainties in the measurement of the momentum and position of a particle are multiplied, the product must be equal to or greater than Planck's constant, h, divided by 2π. The equation looks like this:


(Δp)(Δx) ≥ h/2π


Where Δp is the uncertainty in momentum, Δx is the uncertainty in position, and the product must be equal to or greater than h/2π.
