That's correct! With 90 protons, it's now thorium, a completely different element. We can write this as a nuclear equation:


₉₂²³⁸U → ₉₀²³⁴Th + ₂⁴He

Alia

Great question! In this notation, the bottom number (the subscript) is the atomic number, which tells you the number of protons. The top number (the superscript) is the mass number, which is the total of protons plus neutrons. So uranium-238 has 92 protons and a total mass number of 238, meaning it has 146 neutrons.

₉₀²³⁴Th → ₉₁²³⁴Pa + ₋₁⁰e
