The term electronegativity was coined by Linus Pauling in his famous 1932 publications "The Nature of the Chemical Bond, Parts I–IV." Through these articles, Pauling was the first to formally describe the periodic trends of ionic, polar covalent, and nonpolar covalent bonding.


The term electronegativity refers to the negative charge that an atom in a molecule picks up due to the electrons it may attract. There are other electronegativity scales, but Pauling's original scale has remained the most commonly used.
