Because glass is a polar substance, there are adhesive forces between glass and water. The result is that the water level in the tube rises.


Then, adhesive forces will cause the formation of another steep meniscus. This is followed by the action of cohesive forces, which cause the steep meniscus to be "filled in." This cycle is repeated until the upward adhesive force equals the weight of the raised water in the tube. This rise of the liquid due to the interplay of adhesive and cohesive forces is called capillary action.
